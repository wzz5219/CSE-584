'A heat engine operates between two thermal reservoirs with an efficiency of 99.999%. if lower tem is 273K, what is the higher temp?'	The efficiency of a heat engine is given by:

η = 1 - (T_L / T_H)

where:
* η is the efficiency
* T_L is the absolute temperature of the cold reservoir (in Kelvin)
* T_H is the absolute temperature of the hot reservoir (in Kelvin)

We are given:
* η = 99.999% = 0.99999
* T_L = 273 K

We need to find T_H.  Rearranging the efficiency formula:

T_H = T_L / (1 - η)

Plugging in the values:

T_H = 273 K / (1 - 0.99999)
T_H = 273 K / 0.00001
T_H = 27,300,000 K

So, the higher temperature is $\boxed{27,300,000 \text{ K}}$.
